about Eid-Azha

Eid-ul-Azha, also known as Eid al-Adha or the "Festival of Sacrifice," is an important Islamic holiday celebrated by Muslims worldwide. It commemorates the willingness of the Prophet Ibrahim (Abraham) to sacrifice his son as an act of obedience to God. However, before the sacrifice could take place, God provided a ram to be sacrificed instead. Eid-ul-Azha is observed on the 10th day of Dhu al-Hijjah, the last month of the Islamic lunar calendar. The holiday is marked by special prayers, feasting, and the act of Qurbani, where Muslims who can afford to do so sacrifice an animal (usually a goat, sheep, cow, or camel) and distribute the meat to family, friends, and those in need. The festival is a time of reflection, gratitude, and community, as Muslims come together to celebrate their faith and share with others.
